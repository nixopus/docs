Organizations are the top-level container for everything in Nixopus — projects, apps, domains, and team members all belong to an organization.

## Default organization

Every user gets a default organization when they sign up. You can start deploying immediately without any setup. If you're a solo developer, this is all you need.

## Roles

Each organization member has one of three roles:

| Role | Permissions |
|------|------------|
| **Owner** | Full access. Manage billing, members, and all resources. Cannot be removed. |
| **Admin** | Manage projects, deployments, and domains. Can invite and remove members. |
| **Member** | Deploy apps and manage assigned projects. Cannot change org settings. |

<Note>
  The user who creates an organization is automatically the owner. Ownership cannot be transferred.
</Note>

## Invitations

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Cloud">
    Invite team members by email. They receive a link to join your organization with the role you assign. Invitations expire after 7 days.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Self-hosted">
    Invitations are disabled on self-hosted instances.
    <Info>
      Self-hosted instances are typically used by a single team with direct machine access, so the invitation flow is unnecessary.
    </Info>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
