Every app deployed on Nixopus gets HTTPS out of the box. Custom domains work with automatic SSL certificates — no configuration files, no manual renewal.

<Tip>
  You can manage domains through the [AI Chat](/guides/ai-chat) or the [editor extension](/extension/deploying) — just ask the agent to add or configure a domain and it handles the rest.
</Tip>

## Domain assignment

When creating or configuring an app, you add domains from the **Configuration** tab. Each app supports up to 5 domains.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Single-service apps">
    Add domains directly — each domain routes to your app's exposed port.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Docker Compose apps">
    Each domain maps to a specific **service** and **port** in your Compose file. This lets you route different domains to different services in the same stack.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Automatic HTTPS

Nixopus provisions SSL certificates automatically for every domain:

- **Nixopus Cloud** — certificates are issued at the edge via Let's Encrypt. Zero configuration.
- **Self-hosted** — Caddy handles certificate provisioning and renewal. As long as your machine is reachable on ports 80 and 443, HTTPS works automatically. See [HTTPS configuration](/self-hosting/configuration#https) for details.

## Nixopus Cloud subdomains

On Nixopus Cloud, every app gets a `*.nixopus.ai` subdomain by default. You can also [add a custom domain](/cloud/custom-domains) on Pro, Team, or Enterprise plans.

## Self-hosted domains

For [self-hosted](/self-hosting/installation) installations, point your domain's DNS to your machine's IP address. Nixopus detects the domain and configures the reverse proxy automatically.

The record type depends on your [IP family configuration](/self-hosting/configuration#ipv6-and-dual-stack):

```
# IPv4 (default)
A      app.example.com   →   your-ipv4-address

# IPv6
AAAA   app.example.com   →   your-ipv6-address

# Dual-stack (both)
A      app.example.com   →   your-ipv4-address
AAAA   app.example.com   →   your-ipv6-address
```

<Note>
  Ensure ports 80 and 443 are open on your machine's firewall for SSL certificate issuance to work. See [firewall configuration](/self-hosting/configuration#firewall) for setup instructions.
</Note>
